NCSAEL Publishes Research Work in Renowned Conferences SEKE 2020

NCSAEL has published its research work “ Reed: An Automated Scalable Multi-Language Vulnerability Dataset Generator for Open Source Software” authored by Ahsan Yasin, Dr. Muhammad Faisal Amjad, and Dr. Haider Abbas in “ The 32nd International Conference on Software Engineering & Knowledge Engineering” SEKE 2020. The research proposes a customizable software vulnerability dataset generation tool – Reed. The tool is aimed at generating automated and scalable datasets for any language from open source software. Reed considers the possibility that a single vulnerability may exist in the raw data of multiple software packages and thus makes the generated dataset ideal for machine learning. The results clearly show that the proposed dataset generation method is considerably faster, cheaper, and far more efﬁcient than manual dataset generation which takes many months of effort to create relevant datasets of similar sizes.

NCSAEL Publishes Research Work in One of the Top Conferences SEKE 2020

NCSAEL proudly announces the publication of its research work “An Automated System to Recommend Popular Cybersecurity News using Document Embeddings” written by Research Associate Ramsha Saeed, Dr. Haider Abbas, Sara Asif, Dr. Saddaf Rubab, Dr. Malik M. Khan, and Naima Iltaf in “ The 32nd International Conference on Software Engineering & Knowledge Engineering” SEKE 2020. The research proposes a semantically enriched model which estimates news popularity by measuring cosine similarity between embeddings of the news articles. It further shows that the system based on word2vec trained on cyber news dataset outperforms the other models.

NCSAEL Publishes Research Work In World’s Most Prestigious Journal IEEE COMST (IF 22.973)

NCSAEL proudly announces the publication of its research work entitled “ SS7 Vulnerabilities -A Survey & Implementation of Machine Learning Vs. Rule Based Filtering for Detection of SS7 Network Attacks” authored by Engr Kaleem Ullah, Dr. Imran Rashid, Dr. Hammad Afzal, Asst Prof Waseem Iqbal, Dr. Yawar Abbas Bangash, and Dr. Haider Abbas in “IEEE Communication Surveys & Tutorials” (COMST, IF: 22.973). Published research is oriented around the SS7 attacks with detailed methods to execute attacks, ways to enter the SS7 core network, and recommends countermeasures. It also aims to present a machine learning based framework to detect anomalies in the SS7 network which reveals insights when compared with rule based filtering. The paper is available online at https://doi.org/10.1109/COMST.2020.2971757

SECURITY CHALLENGES IN SOFTWARE DEFINED NETWORKS

The search for better networking paradigms has fostered the emergence of Software Defined Networking (SDN), which is flexible enough to accommodate the ever-growing number of users. SDN offers network operators with significant visibility and granularity in their networks. However, with every new technology paradigm, its security concerns are a serious challenge. In SDN, the control plane is separated from the underlying forwarding plane and so it is prone to many security challenges like Denial of Service (DoS) attack, Distributed DoS (DDoS) attack, Volumetric attack, etc. Join us in an educational conversation on “Security Challenges in SDNs” on 8th October 2020 at 12 PM.
